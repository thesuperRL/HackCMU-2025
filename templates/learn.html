What Are Spotted Lanternflies?

The spotted lanternfly (Lycorma delicatula) is an invasive planthopper native to China and Vietnam. It feeds on plant sap and can harm grapes, apples, hops, and hardwood trees. Its distinctive gray wings with black spots and bright red underwings make it easy to recognize.

Why Are They Bad?

  • Agriculture risk: Damages vineyards, orchards, and timber industries.
  • Tree stress: Heavy feeding weakens plants and invites disease.
  • Honeydew & mold: Excretions promote sooty mold that harms plants and surfaces.
  • Hitchhiking eggs: Egg masses stick to cars, firewood, and gear, accelerating spread.

What You Can Do

  • Report & remove: Stomp nymphs/adults. Scrape egg masses. Each female can lay up to two masses of 50 eggs!
  • Inspect before travel: Check vehicles, outdoor equipment, and firewood.
  • Share knowledge: Teach neighbors and friends how to identify lanternflies and how to deal with them.
  • Log catches: Use the Report page to add a photo and location to the map. Increase your LanternFly Quest score.

Purpose of LanternFly Quest

We turn invasive species control into a shared challenge:

  • Citizen science: Community reports help track infestations in real time.
  • Gamified action: Earn points, build streaks, and climb the leaderboard.
  • Collective impact: Thousands of small actions add up to real ecological protection.

Quick ID Tips

  • Adults: gray wings with black spots; red underwings when flying.

  • Nymphs: black with white spots (early), then red/black with white spots (late).

  • Egg masses: muddy, putty-like smears on trees, vehicles, stones, and outdoor items.
